# Chapter 8: Earth Systems and Resources - References

1. [Plate Tectonics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plate_tectonics) - Wikipedia - Detailed explanation of tectonic plate theory including convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries, the evidence supporting plate movement, and how tectonics shapes Earth's surface over geological time.

2. [Soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive article covering soil formation factors (CLORPT), soil horizons, composition, texture classification, and the critical ecological roles soil plays in supporting terrestrial ecosystems.

3. [Atmospheric Circulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation) - Wikipedia - Explains global wind patterns, Hadley, Ferrel, and polar convection cells, the Coriolis effect, and how uneven solar heating drives planetary-scale air movement and climate zones.

4. Living in the Environment (20th ed.) - Miller & Spoolman - Cengage - Excellent coverage of Earth's physical systems including plate tectonics, soil science, atmospheric layers, weather versus climate, and how these systems interact to create habitat diversity.

5. Environmental Science: Earth as a Living Planet (10th ed.) - Botkin & Keller - Wiley - Strong treatment of geologic processes, soil formation and erosion, watersheds, albedo feedback loops, and the rain shadow effect with clear diagrams and real-world case studies.

6. [Soil Education](https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/conservation-basics/natural-resource-concerns/soils) - USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service - Federal resource on soil science including the Web Soil Survey tool, soil health assessment guides, and educational materials on soil conservation practices.

7. [Plate Tectonics](https://education.nationalgeographic.org/resource/plate-tectonics/) - National Geographic Education - Visually rich educational resource explaining plate boundary types, volcanic activity, earthquake zones, and the ecological significance of tectonic processes with maps and animations.

8. [Climate and Weather](https://www.noaa.gov/education/resource-collections/climate) - NOAA Education - Collection of climate science resources covering atmospheric circulation, seasons, the Coriolis effect, ocean-atmosphere interactions, and climate pattern data with interactive visualizations.

9. [The Water Cycle and Watersheds](https://www.usgs.gov/special-topics/water-science-school) - USGS Water Science School - Interactive educational site explaining watersheds, the hydrologic cycle, groundwater, and surface water systems with data, maps, and activities for students.

10. [Earth Science](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/ap-environmental-science/unit-earth-systems-and-resources) - Khan Academy - Free video lessons and practice exercises covering plate tectonics, soil science, atmospheric layers, global wind patterns, and climate processes aligned with AP Environmental Science standards.
