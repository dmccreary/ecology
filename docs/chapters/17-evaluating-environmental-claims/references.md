# Chapter 17: Evaluating Environmental Claims - References

1. [Greenwashing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenwashing) - Wikipedia - Explains the practice of making misleading environmental claims about products and companies, including common tactics, historical examples, and regulatory responses.

2. [Media Literacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_literacy) - Wikipedia - Overview of the skills needed to critically analyze media messages, including source evaluation, bias detection, and understanding how information ecosystems shape public perception.

3. [Confirmation Bias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confirmation_bias) - Wikipedia - Describes the cognitive tendency to seek and favor information that confirms existing beliefs, with research evidence on how it distorts reasoning about environmental issues.

4. Why Trust Science? - Naomi Oreskes - Princeton University Press - Examines how to distinguish legitimate scientific findings from misinformation and industry-funded doubt campaigns, with case studies from climate and environmental science.

5. Environmental Science (16th ed.) - Miller & Spoolman - Cengage - Covers environmental ethics frameworks, cost-benefit analysis of environmental policies, and citizen science as tools for evaluating and responding to environmental claims.

6. [First Draft: Verification and Media Literacy](https://firstdraftnews.org/) - First Draft - Leading resource on combating misinformation with practical guides for verifying environmental claims, identifying manipulated images, and understanding information disorder.

7. [Media Bias/Fact Check](https://mediabiasfactcheck.com/) - Media Bias/Fact Check - Independent source rating the factual accuracy and political bias of news outlets, useful for evaluating the reliability of media covering environmental topics.

8. [SciCheck at FactCheck.org](https://www.factcheck.org/scicheck/) - Annenberg Public Policy Center - Dedicated science fact-checking project that evaluates false and misleading scientific claims in public discourse, including climate change and environmental health topics.

9. [AllSides Media Bias Ratings](https://www.allsides.com/media-bias) - AllSides - Rates media outlets across the political spectrum and presents multiple perspectives on environmental policy debates, helping readers identify bias in their information diet.

10. [SciStarter: Citizen Science Project Finder](https://scistarter.org/) - SciStarter - Database of over 3,000 citizen science projects searchable by topic, location, and age group, enabling students to participate directly in environmental research and data collection.
