# Chapters

This textbook is organized into 17 chapters covering 380 concepts.

## Chapter Overview

1. [Foundations of Ecology](01-foundations-of-ecology/index.md) - This chapter introduces the core vocabulary and foundational science concepts that underpin all of ecology.
2. [Ecosystems and Biomes](02-ecosystems-and-biomes/index.md) - This chapter surveys the major terrestrial and aquatic biomes on Earth and examines the structural organization of ecosystems.
3. [Energy Flow in Ecosystems](03-energy-flow/index.md) - This chapter examines how energy enters, moves through, and exits ecosystems.
4. [Biogeochemical Cycles](04-biogeochemical-cycles/index.md) - This chapter traces how matter cycles through Earth's living and nonliving systems.
5. [Species Interactions](05-species-interactions/index.md) - This chapter explores the ecological relationships between organisms, from predation and competition to mutualism and parasitism.
6. [Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services](06-biodiversity-and-services/index.md) - This chapter examines biodiversity at the species and ecosystem levels, and the essential services that healthy ecosystems provide to humans.
7. [Population Ecology](07-population-ecology/index.md) - This chapter covers how populations grow, stabilize, and decline, from single-species models to human demographic patterns.
8. [Earth Systems and Resources](08-earth-systems/index.md) - This chapter examines the physical foundations of ecology: how plate tectonics shapes habitats, how soils form and erode, how the atmosphere drives weather patterns, and how solar radiation creates climate zones.
9. [Sustainability and Energy Resources](09-sustainability-and-energy/index.md) - This chapter introduces the concept of sustainability and surveys the full spectrum of energy resources available to human civilization.
10. [Land and Water Use](10-land-and-water-use/index.md) - This chapter examines how humans use land and water resources and the ecological consequences of those choices.
11. [Atmospheric Pollution](11-atmospheric-pollution/index.md) - This chapter covers the sources, chemistry, and health effects of air pollution.
12. [Water and Land Pollution](12-water-and-land-pollution/index.md) - This chapter examines water and land pollution, from nutrient runoff and eutrophication to persistent organic pollutants and bioaccumulation.
13. [Systems Thinking](13-systems-thinking/index.md) - This chapter develops the thinking tools that ecologists use to understand complex, interconnected systems.
14. [Scientific Literacy](14-scientific-literacy/index.md) - This chapter builds the skills students need to evaluate scientific claims.
15. [Global Climate Change](15-global-climate-change/index.md) - This chapter addresses the science of global climate change, from the greenhouse effect and ozone depletion to ocean warming, acidification, and sea level rise.
16. [Biodiversity Loss and Policy](16-biodiversity-loss-and-policy/index.md) - This chapter examines the causes of biodiversity loss and the policy frameworks designed to prevent extinction.
17. [Evaluating Environmental Claims](17-evaluating-environmental-claims/index.md) - This capstone chapter applies all prior knowledge to the practical challenge of evaluating environmental claims encountered in social media, news, and everyday life.

## How to Use This Textbook

Chapters are ordered so that prerequisite concepts always appear before the concepts that depend on them. You can follow the chapters in order for a complete learning path, or use the learning graph viewer to find specific concepts and identify which chapters you need to complete first.

---

**Note:** Each chapter includes a list of concepts covered. Make sure to complete prerequisites before moving to advanced chapters.
