# Chapter 6: Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services - References

## Annotated References

1. [Biodiversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive article covering genetic, species, and ecosystem diversity, measurement methods including the Shannon index, and the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem function. Discusses current extinction rates and conservation challenges.

2. [Ecosystem Services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem_services) - Wikipedia - Detailed overview of the four categories of ecosystem services (provisioning, regulating, cultural, and supporting), their economic valuation, and the Millennium Ecosystem Assessment framework. Explains why healthy ecosystems are essential to human well-being.

3. [Island Biogeography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insular_biogeography) - Wikipedia - Thorough treatment of MacArthur and Wilson's theory of island biogeography, the species-area relationship, and equilibrium species richness. Covers applications to habitat fragmentation and conservation reserve design beyond literal islands.

4. Ecology: Concepts and Applications (9th ed.) - Manuel Molles - McGraw-Hill - Excellent chapters on biodiversity measurement, ecosystem services valuation, and ecological succession. Includes worked examples of the species-area relationship and clear diagrams of primary and secondary succession stages.

5. The Economy of Nature (8th ed.) - Robert Ricklefs & Rick Relyea - W.H. Freeman - Strong coverage of island biogeography theory, ecological tolerance curves, and succession dynamics. Includes quantitative treatment of species diversity indices and the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem stability.

6. [Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/ap-biology/ecology-ap/biodiversity-and-ecosystem-services/a/intro-to-biodiversity) - Khan Academy - Free lessons on species diversity, ecosystem services, and threats to biodiversity. Clear explanations of species richness versus evenness, with practice exercises on interpreting diversity data and evaluating conservation strategies.

7. [Ecosystem Services](https://education.nationalgeographic.org/resource/ecosystem-services/) - National Geographic Education - Accessible overview of how ecosystems provide food, clean water, climate regulation, and cultural benefits to humans. Includes real-world examples and infographics showing the economic value of natural services for high school learners.

8. [Ecological Succession](https://www.britannica.com/science/ecological-succession) - Britannica - Detailed article on primary and secondary succession, pioneer species, climax communities, and the modern understanding of successional dynamics. Covers classic examples including Mount St. Helens recovery and old-field succession.

9. [Biodiversity: Crash Course Ecology](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GK_vRtHJZu4) - Crash Course (YouTube) - Engaging video covering species diversity, ecosystem services, island biogeography, and threats to biodiversity. Uses clear animations to explain the species-area relationship and why biodiversity loss affects ecosystem function.

10. [The Value of the World's Ecosystem Services](https://www.nature.com/scitable/knowledge/library/ecosystem-services-702) - Nature Education (Scitable) - Peer-reviewed educational articles on ecosystem service valuation, the relationship between biodiversity and ecosystem function, and approaches to conservation prioritization. Connects ecological theory to real-world policy and management decisions.
