# Chapter 10: Land and Water Use - References

1. [Tragedy of the Commons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tragedy_of_the_commons) - Wikipedia - Explains Garrett Hardin's influential concept of shared resource depletion, critiques of the model, Elinor Ostrom's Nobel Prize-winning research on commons governance, and real-world examples.

2. [Deforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deforestation) - Wikipedia - Comprehensive overview of global deforestation causes, rates, and ecological consequences including habitat loss, carbon emissions, soil erosion, and disrupted hydrological cycles across tropical, temperate, and boreal forests.

3. [Integrated Pest Management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_pest_management) - Wikipedia - Describes the IPM approach combining biological, cultural, mechanical, and chemical pest control methods, the economic threshold concept, and how IPM reduces pesticide dependence while maintaining crop yields.

4. Living in the Environment (20th ed.) - Miller & Spoolman - Cengage - Thorough coverage of agriculture, deforestation, mining, urbanization, and sustainable alternatives including crop rotation, no-till farming, aquaculture, and green infrastructure with case studies.

5. Environmental Science: Earth as a Living Planet (10th ed.) - Botkin & Keller - Wiley - Strong treatment of land use impacts, irrigation problems including salinization and aquifer depletion, CAFO environmental effects, and overfishing with quantitative data and policy analysis.

6. [Sustainable Agriculture](https://www.usda.gov/topics/organic) - USDA - Federal resources on sustainable farming practices including organic certification, conservation programs, crop rotation guidelines, integrated pest management, and soil health initiatives for American agriculture.

7. [Deforestation and Forest Degradation](https://www.worldwildlife.org/threats/deforestation-and-forest-degradation) - World Wildlife Fund - Educational resource on global deforestation drivers, biodiversity impacts, community effects, and conservation solutions including FSC-certified sustainable forestry and protected area management.

8. [Overfishing](https://www.fao.org/fishery/en/collection/sofia) - UN Food and Agriculture Organization - The State of World Fisheries and Aquaculture report providing authoritative global data on fish stocks, overfishing trends, bycatch, aquaculture growth, and sustainable fisheries management.

9. [Urban Heat Island Effect](https://www.epa.gov/green-infrastructure) - U.S. Environmental Protection Agency - EPA resources on green infrastructure solutions for urban runoff, heat island mitigation, permeable pavement, rain gardens, and green roofs with implementation guides and case studies.

10. [Water Resources](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources) - U.S. Geological Survey - Comprehensive data and educational resources on groundwater, aquifer depletion, irrigation water use, watershed management, and water quality monitoring across the United States.
