## Full Version

Excited to share a new open educational resource: an interactive intelligent textbook on Ecology designed for high school students (grades 9-12)!

This textbook takes a systems thinking approach to ecology, helping students see not just individual parts of the natural world but the patterns, feedback loops, and emergent behaviors that arise when those parts interact. Every chapter builds critical thinking and media literacy skills so students can evaluate environmental claims they encounter in social media and the news.

Built using MkDocs Material and AI-assisted content generation with Claude, this open-source project demonstrates how AI can augment educational content creation while maintaining pedagogical rigor.

By the numbers:

- 17 chapters covering ecosystems, biodiversity, population ecology, earth systems, pollution, climate change, systems thinking, and scientific literacy
- 380 concepts organized in an interactive dependency learning graph
- 83 interactive MicroSims (p5.js, Chart.js, Plotly, vis-network)
- 170 quiz questions aligned to Bloom's Taxonomy
- 381 glossary terms with precise definitions
- 102 frequently asked questions
- 82 diagrams and visual elements
- 322,000+ total words (~1,350 equivalent printed pages)
- 17 graphic novel stories about scientists and environmental pioneers
- Bailey the Beaver, a pedagogical agent who guides students with humor and encouragement

Every chapter includes embedded interactive simulations, mascot-guided learning, critical thinking challenges, and misinformation detection exercises. All content is freely available under a Creative Commons CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license.

Explore the full textbook: https://dmccreary.github.io/ecology/

GitHub: https://github.com/dmccreary/ecology
